Being Useful

2020-06-26

I used to work myself to death. Not resting, I would try to complete as many tasks as possible each day. Organized todo lists, itemized jira boards, and productivity hacks littered my second monitor as I did my best to cram as much work into each day as I could. By the end of the day, I felt exhausted. I thought this was a sign I had a productive day.

Much to my surprise, even though I felt like I was killing myself, I was passed over for promotion a couple of times. When asking my manager for feedback, I was told I did a good job and didn't have much to pick at. I felt like I was a top performer. I mean, hell, I was busting my ass every day to do as much work as possible. Even so, I saw others around me promoted on a faster time line and with more benefits.

I didn't know it at the time, but there was a deeper issue with my work performance I hadn't grokked yet.
