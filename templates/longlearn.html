Incremental Learning

During my school years I forgot how to learn. Whether it was due to my teachers or the system itself, I lost the ability to teach myself.

Each year I felt like I was on some kind of obsessive treadmill. While school was in, I was always looking forward to the weekend or vacation. When vacation happened, I would immerse myself with binge-like behaviors until I felt bored with them. After feeling that boredom, I looked forward to having something to explore or learn. I never found what it was.

As my school years dragged on, I felt that something was wrong with me. I felt like I wasn't smart enough or curious enough to succeed.

Following this loss, my love for life also diminished. A feeling of hopelessness crawled into the back of my head. In high school, I spent many a plan-less Friday night grappling with a feeling of anxiety. It felt like the whole world was passing me by and there was nothing I could do to stop it. I couldn't figure out what to do.
