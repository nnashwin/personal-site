Finding a Client

2020-07-01

My first client came through a friend. I had met a guy who started his own business in Taiwan and wanted to hire a software engineer.

After interviewing for the job, it was determined I didn't quite have enough experience to fill the position. We became friends instead.

Through the next year and a half, he introduced me to a lot of people. Job interviews, social events, and a freelancing gig followed. He was a great friend.

I didn't understand it at the time, but a lot of the tech business is done this way. Meet people. Make friends. Dream ideas. Share them. Work together. Hopefully sell them. Meet other people.

But I was still stuck in a more traditional mindset. I didn't know how to keep in contact with friends. I moved back to the US and dropped out of contact with him.

"Networking" as Making Friends

Recently, I find myself back in Taiwan. I reconnected with some old friends. I also realized that as I have learned more, I have more to offer. On a whim, and after a thorough reconnecting, I reached out to one of my friends to ask if they had something they want to build.
